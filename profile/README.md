## 🚀 Summary
iZotope Ozone for Windows is a mastering environment that helps you take a mix across the finish line. It combines the essential mastering stages — equalization, dynamics, stereo imaging, and loudness maximizing — into one workspace, and its assistive, AI-guided analysis can suggest a starting chain to build from. You can run Ozone standalone for focused mastering sessions or load it as a plugin on the master bus in your DAW. The lineage runs from earlier releases like izotope ozone 9 through izotope ozone 11 and izotope ozone 12, with editions such as izotope ozone 12 elements at the entry level.

## ✨ Feature Set
1. Assistive, AI-guided analysis that proposes a starting master 🤖
2. A mastering equalizer for precise tonal balance 🎛️
3. Multiband dynamics for level control and cohesion 📊
4. Per-band stereo imaging, with the izotope ozone imager also offered free 🎧
5. A transparent maximizer and metering to reach loudness targets 🔊

## 🎯 Getting Started
1. Open Ozone standalone, or insert it on the master bus of a session in your DAW.
2. Play the mix and run the assistive analysis to get a suggested chain.
3. Adjust EQ, dynamics, and imaging until the balance is right.
4. Set a loudness target with the maximizer and watch the meters.
5. Export the master and A/B it against reference tracks.

## ❓ FAQ
**Is iZotope Ozone free?**
The complete Ozone suite is a paid product available in several editions, usually with a trial so you can test it first. iZotope also offers the Ozone Imager plugin as a free download, giving you a no-cost way to explore stereo imaging without the full suite.

**Which versions of Windows are supported?**
iZotope Ozone for Windows supports current 64-bit versions of Windows, including Windows 10 and Windows 11. The latest stable release offers the broadest compatibility.

**Is my data secure?**
Your audio is processed locally on your own machine during mastering, and account or activation is handled through official iZotope services. Your project files remain in your storage.
